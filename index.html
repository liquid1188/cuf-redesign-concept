Information Services

Information Services is the backbone of CUF.

From its beginnings, CUF provided answers to any questions about the Catholic Church; questions about doctrine, discipline, or even current events. Anyone may contact CUF and ask any question pertaining to the Catholic Church. If IS does not have an answer for you, the staff will research your question and provide you with an answer.

Answers are given in writing, either as a Faith Fact or as a research letter. This allows CUF to provide substantive magisterial and scriptural citations that you can reference later. CUF doesn't just explain what the Church teaches or does, we also explain why and how to live the Faith.

Our Story

A defense begun in 1968.

In the wake of Vatican II, many Catholics were confused. Many were in error. A courageous group of laymen banded together in 1968 to defend the Truth. The Mystical Body of Christ was under attack then and remains under attack now. Our Mother still needs to be defended.

1968

A new apostolate is born

Matters came to a head with the publication of Pope Paul VI's Humanae Vitae and the immediate wave of dissent it garnered. In September of 1968, H. Lyman Stebbins was chosen to serve as the founding president of CUF.

1970

Lay Witness begins

CUF began publishing its Lay Witness newsletter more than 50 years ago — what began as a newsletter for members developed into a full-color magazine, and continues today.

1984

A sister apostolate

Chuck Wilson founded CUF's sister apostolate, The Saint Joseph Foundation, to assist the Faithful in the vindication of their rights within the Law of the Catholic Church.

1986

Luther in reverse

Dr. Scott Hahn was introduced to his local CUF chapter in Milwaukee. St. Gregory VII Chapter of CUF is still meeting in Milwaukee, and Scott Hahn is still Catholic.

1987

Faith and Life

CUF published the renowned Faith and Life catechism series for grades 1–8, now in its third edition and widely used by parishes, Catholic schools, and homeschooling families.

1998

FOCUS and Emmaus Road

Under president Curtis Martin, CUF launched the Fellowship of Catholic University Students, founded that year as its own organization, and started Emmaus Road Publishing in response to the call for a new evangelization.

2014

A painful transition

The President recommended that the apostolate wind down operations and dissolve. The Board of Directors rejected the proposal, and a difficult transition began to preserve CUF's mission.

2017

Two sisters under one roof

CUF affiliated to its longtime sister apostolate, The Saint Joseph Foundation. CUF remains an autonomous apostolate, sharing office space and an exceptional research library.

2018

Golden Anniversary

CUF celebrated 50 years of uniting the Faithful from all walks of life to support, defend, and advance the Teachings of the Catholic Church.

Today

A threshold of Hope

Every day, CUF is connecting Truth-seekers and devout Catholics with the information, resources, and community they need to grow in the Faith and be instruments of renewal.

The Mission

What CUF is.

Catholics United for the Faith (CUF) is an international lay apostolate founded to unite the Faithful from all walks of life in order to support, defend, and advance the Teaching Office of the Catholic Church. CUF is faithful to the Magisterium of the Church, holding that the Church is indefectible and will not fail, that Her teachings can develop but never fundamentally change.

CUF seeks to support the hierarchy in proclaiming the saving truths, but is not afraid to warn the faithful against error and immorality wherever it appears. CUF is committed to assisting Catholics of all states in life to participate in the Church's apostolic activity, reform existing activities, and even found their own apostolates where this is needed. In these ways, CUF pursues and supports authentic Church Reform.

"In times of confusion and uncertainty, the CUF chapter and all CUF members strive to be a source of stability by bearing clear witness to the authentic doctrines and disciplines of the Church."
From the CUF Chapters charter
CUF Membership

Unite your voice with thousands.

As a lay apostolate, Catholics United for the Faith provides a cooperative structure through which the laity can be effective in their vocation. While an individual might have limited resources, the combined resources of thousands united in effort bring about a greater effect. When an individual seeks to express himself, he remains but one voice. Combined with others in an apostolate, his voice is amplified.

Members first seek personal holiness, so that they are ready instruments for evangelistic action.

Become a member
